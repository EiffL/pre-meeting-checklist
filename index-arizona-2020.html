Checklist for the LSST DESC Meeting (Arizona 2020)

Hereâ€™s a few useful things you can do to prepare yourself for the upcoming DESC meeting at UA!

Check out and bookmark the meeting agenda page, on both your laptop and phone. Make sure you remember your Confluence password.
If you are preparing a presentation for the meeting, you can use our DESC templates. Remember to upload or provide links to your presentation materials on the appropriate session pages on Confluence before the session!
Join the Slack channel #desc-collab-meeting to hear announcements and to chat with meeting participants (both in-person and virtual). Hack Day participants should also join #desc-hack-sprint.
Make sure you have a NERSC account and remember your password (nope, a SSH key is not enough) and have 2-step verification (MFA) set up! Need to apply for one? Forget your password? Questions? Ask them in the #desc-nersc Slack channel. Also, make sure you are in the "lsst" group. If not, contact Richard Dubois or Debbie Bard to request to be added to the "lsst" group.
Follow the instruction to install DESC jupyter kernels. You will need them to access DC2 data products or to use DESC software during the Collaboration meeting.
If you have not used any DC2 data tutorial notebooks before, check out the instruction on this step-by-step guide. Also, bookmark the DC2 Data Product Overview page and the DC2-analysis GitHub repo for easy access. Questions? Ask them in the #desc-dc2-users Slack channel.
(For DE School participants) Check out the pre-class materials for each lesson on the DE School page. Remember to add your name to the participant list at the bottom of the DE School page too.
(For Sprint Day participants) Visit the Sprint Day page to browse and add your hack/sprint ideas! Remember, not all hacks and sprints have to be carried out, so be bold when adding ideas! Also add your name and departure time at the bottom at the page!
(For virtual participants) Zoom connection instructions are consolidated on the agenda page (below the physical room names). If you want to be heard in plenary sessions, please "raise your hand" in Zoom or use the #desc-collab-meeting Slack channel. For parallel sessions, please contact the session organizers for help!
Remind yourself of our Code of Conduct and the Virtual Meeting Best Practices. Note that Code of Conduct applies to virtual participants too and many of the Best Practices apply to in-person participants as well!
Having technical difficulties with Slack, Confluence, GitHub, or NERSC? Check out our Tech Cheat Sheet and bookmark it!
Check out the logistics page or the public meeting page for travel tips.
If you use social media professionally, please use the #lsstdesc hashtag when reporting highlights from the meeting!

This site uses cookies to keep track your checklist progress. The data are stored in your browser locally only.
