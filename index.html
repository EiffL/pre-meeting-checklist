Checklist for the Upcoming LSST DESC Meeting

Hereâ€™s a few useful things you can do to prepare yourself for the upcoming DESC meeting at CMU!

Check out and bookmark the meeting agenda page, on both your laptop and phone. Make sure you remember your Confluence password.
If you are preparing a presentation for the meeting, you can use our DESC templates. Remember to upload or provide links to your presentation materials on the appropriate session pages on Confluence before the session!
Join the Slack channel #desc-collab-meeting to hear announcements and to chat with meeting participants (both in-person and virtual). Hack Day participants should also join #desc-hack-sprint.
Make sure you have a NERSC account and remember your password (nope, a SSH key is not enough)! Need to apply for one? Forget your password? Questions? Ask them in the #desc-nersc Slack channel.
Run 
ssh -t <user>@cori.nersc.gov groups
to make sure you are in the "lsst" group. If not, contact Richard Dubois or Debbie Bard to request to be added to the "lsst" group.
Follow the instruction to install DESC jupyter kernels. You will need them during the Collaboration meeting.
Check out and bookmark the DC2 Data Product Overview page and the DC2 GitHub repo. Both of them have links for many useful tutorial materials.
(For DE School participants) Check out the pre-class materials for each lesson on the DE School page. Also, to make the most of the 3x2 lesson, consider installing the Core Cosmology Library (CCL) on your laptop or familiarising yourself with the NERSC installation. Questions? Ask them in the #desc-ccl Slack channel.
(For Hack Day participants) Visit the Hack Day page to browse and add your hack/sprint ideas! Remember, not all hacks and sprints have to be carried out, so be bold when adding ideas! Also add your name and departure time at the bottom at the page!
(For virtual participants) Zoom connection instructions are consolidated on the Zoom page. If you want to be heard in plenary sessions, please "raise your hand" in Zoom or use the #desc-collab-meeting Slack channel. For parallel sessions, please contact the session organizers for help!
Remind yourself of our Code of Conduct and the Virtual Meeting Best Practices. Note that Code of Conduct applies to virtual participants too and many of the Best Practices apply to in-person participants as well!
Want to be free from technical difficulties? Check out our Tech Cheat Sheet and bookmark it!
If you will be driving or will need a ride, visit the carpooling page to provide/request rides.
If you use social media professionally, please use the #lsstdesc hashtag when reporting highlights from the meeting!

This site uses cookies to keep track your checklist progress. The data are stored in your browser locally only.
